Our Story

Where Tradition
Meets Today

Nestled in the heart of Chapel Hill on Franklin Street, TAAL is more than a restaurant — it is a celebration of rhythm, flavour, and culture. The word taal means rhythm in Hindi, and every dish, every evening is composed like music — layered, intentional, deeply felt.

We bring the finest traditions of Indian cuisine into a contemporary space, with ingredients chosen for their stories and techniques that honour generations of culinary artistry. From notes of cardamom in our small plates to the harmony of the season in our chef's selections, each plate is a composition.

Located on Chapel Hill's iconic Franklin Street, TAAL is proud to be the first upscale Indian dining destination in the Triangle's most vibrant neighbourhood — 20 minutes from Research Triangle Park, at the heart of it all.

The Name

ताल  Taal

In the language of Indian classical music, taal is the rhythmic cycle — the pulse that underlies every raga, every composition, every moment of silence between notes. It is structure and freedom at once; ancient and alive.

At our table, every dish is composed like music — layered, intentional, deeply felt. The spice follows a pattern. The silence between courses is deliberate. You are not just dining; you are listening.

The Pantry

Ingredients of India

A rotating spotlight on the spices at the heart of our kitchen

Kerala, South India · Elettaria cardamomum

Cardamom

Called the "Queen of Spices," green cardamom carries a floral, eucalyptus warmth that brightens everything it touches. At TAAL, you'll find it in our Masala Chai, in the gulab jamun soufflé, and woven into our house-smoked paneer tikka.

Kashmir Valley · Crocus sativus

Saffron

The world's most precious spice by weight, hand-harvested from the stamens of the crocus flower. Kashmiri saffron brings a honeyed warmth and golden colour to our dum biryanis, the Saffron Negroni, and the mango panna cotta.

Tamil Nadu, South India · Tamarindus indica

Tamarind

Tangy, complex, and deeply savoury — tamarind is the backbone of chutneys, chaats, and the Mango Taal cocktail. We use whole tamarind pods, slow-soaked, in our house gastrique and the Jaljeera Sparkling.

Chef Portrait

The Composer

Chef Arjun Mehta

Trained in the kitchens of the Taj in Mumbai, Chef Arjun Mehta refined his craft under two Michelin-starred chefs in London and New York before returning to the flavours of his childhood — reimagined for the contemporary table.

At TAAL, his cuisine honours India's culinary heritage while embracing the ingredients of the Carolina landscape: heirloom grains from local farms, seafood from the Carolina coast, and spices sourced directly from small producers in Kerala, Kashmir, and Tamil Nadu.

"Every dish should feel like a memory you haven't had yet."

Arjun Mehta
